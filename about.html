To crystallize an economic policy that will gradually get rid of the economic dependence .1 and dependency on the occupying countries, which is based on the limitations and restrictions of the “Oslo” agreements and the “Paris” economic protocol.

We will legislate that protects the capabilities and resources of the Palestinian people .2, ensures the equitable distribution of national revenues and wealth, and preserves the right of future generations.

Develop a social economy based on production that provides wide areas of operation .3 and investment that contribute to addressing the problems of poverty and unemployment, and increasing income.

Regulating the market economy according to the rules and provisions of fair competitors .4 in a way that prevents monopoly and exploitation, meets the basic needs of citizens and achieves balance and justice.

Restructuring the public budget on the basis of priority in the allocation of resources, and .5 commitment to rationalization plans and standards of integrity, transparency and accountability in the management of public money.

8. Seeking to build fruitful economic relations with the Arab and Islamic oceans, which pushes the pace of development, and opens up prospects for investment and trade exchange and new markets for work.

9. A “multi-dimensional” development strategy that allows for the restructuring of the Palestinian economy in a way that is compatible with the determinants of the stages of national liberation.

Third: Building a vibrant society, in which human dignity is preserved, and the elements of quality of life are available. A vision based on the “future” for building a vibrant society depends on the positive interaction of several ethical, social, cultural, political, economic and demographic factors, as follows:

1. Activating the laws that protect the dignity, rights and freedoms of the citizen.

2. Strengthening the Palestinian identity and advancing the national culture.

3. Enhancing the education of responsible citizens and the culture of tolerance, as an alternative to hate speech and blind partisanship.

4. Combat and suffice all forms of discrimination that contradict the foundations of social justice.

5. Enhancing the participatory approach between societal forces, civil society institutions, and individuals, as a vital input to the development and cohesion of society.

6. Improving the “preventive and curative” health care system, and equitable dissemination of health services throughout the country.

7. Develop the education and teaching systems, higher education, and scientific research institutions, in a manner consistent with the Palestinian reality and its aspirations for the future.

8. Developing an integrated housing system that includes the enactment of legislation and policies, a national fund for housing financing, and government lands at affordable and affordable prices for families.

9. Develop the system of trade union work, legislation, policies, and working mechanisms: ensuring its democratization, independence, regulatory effectiveness, and social role.

10. Develop public policies that support the rights of the Palestinian child.

11. Protecting the rights of the families of martyrs, prisoners and the wounded, and the rights of people with disabilities.

12. Develop the social security system to ensure the reduction of poverty and social gaps, and launch unified portals for social assistance.

13. Expanding the scope of cultural, social, voluntary, sports and creative activities, allowing more community interaction and achieving well-being.

3. Namaa system: It includes various sustainable development programs, entrepreneurship training, support for youth initiatives, support for quality projects, support for innovation and patents, and facilitation of job opportunities in the Arab labor market.
